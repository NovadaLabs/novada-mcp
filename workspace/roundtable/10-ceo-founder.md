# CEO/Founder Perspective

## 1. Building or Selling?

We are building. We should be selling.

The 50-agent sprint, 30 bug fixes, 14-chapter docs -- that is engineering theater disguised as progress. Zero paying customers after 17 projects is not a product problem. It is a distribution problem. The product benchmarks well. Nobody knows it exists.

Ratio should flip to 80% selling, 20% building. Building only what a specific prospect needs to close.

## 2. The ONE Metric for 30 Days

**First 5 paying users.** Not signups. Not npm downloads. Users who pay money.

Everything else is vanity. 1,253 npm downloads means nothing if none convert. Firecrawl has 494K because they solved distribution first, not features.

## 3. STOP Immediately

- **Stop multi-agent architecture sprints.** The infra is sufficient. Polish does not acquire customers.
- **Stop internal tooling (AAM, Plywood, AgentRecall improvements).** These serve us, not customers. Pause until revenue exists.
- **Stop building features nobody asked for.** Every new tool should trace to a conversation with a real prospect.

The product is not the bottleneck. Our unwillingness to do the uncomfortable work of selling is. Ship what we have. Find 5 people who will pay for it this week. Fix what breaks for them. That is the entire plan.
